EU referendum broke code of good practice

Posted on 30 Jan 2020 One min read

Referendum result ‘unsafe’

Leave.eu has been fined the maximum amount possible by the Electoral Commission for multiple breaches of electoral rules. Other leave organisations have also been convicted of breaking electoral law.

Given that the UK is one of the 61 member states of the Commission and helped to form the Code, which was adopted in 2006. Proper adoption of this code would enable the Electoral Commision to annul an election or referendum if serious irregularities may have affected the result. Which is clearly the case for the 2016 Referendum. However, currently the Electoral Commision does not have that power.
